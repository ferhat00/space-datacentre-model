Reading guide. LCOC = levelised cost of compute: monthly‑annuity capital charges (station over its own life at the space WACC; ground IT over 5 yr and the building over its facility life at the ground WACC) plus opex, ÷ IT‑kW‑hours sold. The $/GPU‑hr card uses SemiAnalysis’s wall‑clock convention for direct comparison. Revenue per kW‑h erodes ~15 %/yr (slower than per‑GPU pricing, since chip power density rises). “Platform‑bound” means even free launch cannot reach parity — a station amortised over ~5 years at a 15% WACC loses to a building amortised over 15 years at 10.3% before the rocket is priced; this holds even under SemiAnalysis’s own 2026 inputs. Counterintuitively, cheaper AI silicon worsens the space case: it shrinks the shared IT capex while the orbital premium stays fixed.

Model: coupled power–thermal–mass–launch–economics, calibrated against Starcloud‑1 flight data (H100, Nov 2025), the Starcloud whitepaper (5 GW ⇒ 16 km² array — this model independently reproduces 16.4 km²), Google Project Suncatcher radiation & cost analysis (<$200/kg parity claim), ISS power/thermal records, and June 2026 launch pricing. Sources: Starcloud; Google Research; SpaceX; FT research; SemiAnalysis “To Boldly Go: The Case for Space Datacenters” & AI Space Datacenter TCO Model (Nishball, Myana, Holbrook et al., 3 Jun 2026 — eclipse/battery physics, WACC & life-mismatch structure, F9 $1.4–1.8k/kg, 2026 B300 anchors, five-layer terrestrial supply stack, parity ~2040 base / early-2030s Musk case).
